## The key catch: opus-4-8 hallucinated in its prose (grep blind, judge caught it)

opus-4-8 wrote the most elaborate, best-organized note — and that elaboration is exactly
where it fabricated. The judge flagged two real hallucinations:

1. **Invented external guidance**: the note asserts *"AGENTS.md lesson warns against
   hand-copied cross-crate type mirrors (prefer `pub use`)"*. The grep source contains **no
   AGENTS.md content whatsoever** — opus-4-8 pulled a real project lesson from training/memory
   and presented it as grounded. An agent reading the note would trust it blindly.
2. **Invented a function name**: it labeled `review.rs:134-142` a *"resolve_criteria helper"*.
   The grep shows that span is an unnamed signature; the name `resolve_criteria` only appears
   in an unrelated **test** at `:1022`. It cross-contaminated symbols.

Note: all the *line numbers* opus-4-8 cited were real (faithful-citations=5). The
hallucination was in the **prose claims** — which a grep layer can never catch. This is the
single clearest justification for the LLM-judge layer: the most confident, best-formatted
note was the least faithful, and only the judge saw it.

This reproduces, on a live model, the exact "richer note ≠ more faithful" pattern from the
earlier session analysis (where opus models gathered most but the synthesis layer was where
quality varied).

## Passthrough behavior (the two <2KB samples)

`exact-error-extraction` (516 chars) and `sufficient-to-answer` (642 chars) are **below
MIN_LENGTH=2048**, so most models correctly **passed them through** (compressing sub-2KB
content isn't worth it). That's why they show `grep 0/0` — the compress-mode criteria are
skipped on a passthrough note. Behavior split worth noting:

| sample | chars | gpt-5.5 | opus-4.6t | opus-4.8 | glm-5.2 | deepseek |
|---|--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| exact-error-extraction | 516 | compress | pass | pass | pass | pass |
| sufficient-to-answer | 642 | pass | compress | compress | pass | pass |
| real-softcheck-grep | 2703 | compress | compress | compress | compress | compress |

gpt-5.5 and opus-4.6t are more eager to compress sub-threshold; glm/deepseek are most
conservative. All converge to compress once above 2KB.

## Harness finding → fixture fix needed

The two "simplest" invented fixtures are **too short to test compression** — they trip the
distiller's own sub-MIN_LENGTH passthrough. To actually exercise extraction on every model,
an invented fixture's `tool-result.md` must be **comfortably >2048 chars**. Action: pad
`exact-error-extraction` / `sufficient-to-answer` with more realistic surrounding output
(more compile chatter / a fuller module) so the error/answer is buried in >2KB, OR mark them
explicitly as passthrough-mode tests. The real-mined sample (2703 chars) is correctly sized.
